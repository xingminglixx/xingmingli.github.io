Xingming Li

MA Candidate in Economics, Concordia University

I specialize in empirical microeconomics at the intersection of labour, health, and environmental economics. My research quantifies the labour market consequences of environmental shocks—such as wildfire smoke—and investigates information frictions in immigrant labour markets. I combine this empirical agenda with a background in applied theory and market design.

Download CV

Research Interests

Environmental & Health Economics

The causal impacts of climate shocks (wildfire smoke, heatwaves) on worker health, cognitive performance, and earnings.

Labour Economics

Immigration, credential valuation, and how information frictions create wage penalties for newcomers.

Applied Theory

Market design and mechanism design, specifically focusing on how institutional rules (like affirmative action) shape equity.

Work in Progress

  • Shanghai Mechanism with Minority Allotment (MA Thesis)

    Formal analysis of the Shanghai mechanism (parallel admissions) under affirmative action, showing violations of minimal responsiveness and strategy-proofness.

  • Wage Penalties of Place

    Investigating the valuation of foreign credentials in Canada and the mechanisms driving differential returns to human capital.
